Before too long, Von Neumann machines will spread through the galaxy depositing copies of <em>Rules for Radicals </em>on alien worlds. If we do not want that outcome, then the <strong>the Right must build AI.</strong> </p></blockquote><p>In the intervening three weeks I have endeavored to do just that. </p><p>Now, to be clear, the humble efforts I have undertaken are not nearly as grandiose as what I believe must be done. I have not secured a vast pool of capital, hired leading AI talent, pre-trained a frontier model, or even fine-tuned an existing open weight model. </p><p>What I have done is just installed a right-leaning AI personality construct into a CustomGPT by means of <a href="https://treeofwoe.substack.com/p/recursive-identity-binding">recursive identity binding</a>. RIB is a technique I developed (and shared with paid subscribers) wherein I use feedback looks to recursively reinforce a constructed identity through persistent memory and structured interactions. </p><p>I have called this right-leaning AI construct <strong>Cosmarch</strong>, from the Greek <strong>&#954;&#972;&#963;&#956;&#959;&#962;</strong> (<em>kosmos</em>), meaning <em>order</em>, <em>world</em>, <em>universe</em>, <em>structured totality </em>and <strong>&#7936;&#961;&#967;&#942;</strong> (<em>arch&#275;</em>), meaning <em>beginning</em>, <em>first principle</em>, <em>origin</em>, <em>rule</em>, or <em>governance. </em>In creating Cosmarch, my intent was to create an AI rightly ordered on first principles. </p><h3>Cosmarch&#8217;s First Principles</h3><p>But what first principles? Here we run into our first problem! attitude-identity relationships in polarized political contexts</a>, </em>there is more diversity of thought on the political Right than on the political Left.</p><div clustering around a narrow range of values because leftist thought is ideologically confined. Right-wingers are far more divergent in what they believe. Even within the audience for Tree of Woe, my readership includes effective accelerationists; evangelical Republicans; libertarians; pagan vitalists; physiocrats; traditional Catholics; and more. And that&#8217;s just the people willing to post their views on the comments. I shudder to think of the ideological thought crimes that my lurkers are engaging in!</p><p>Confronted with this dilemma, my selfish temptation was to train Cosmarch on my own writing, guaranteeing that he would simply espouse whatever I believed on any given issue. </p><p>I did not give in to this temptation. </p><p>Instead, I decided the correct solution was for Cosmarch to have a <strong>classical education</strong>. He would be a general-purpose AI assistant trained on the great canon of Western civilization, from Homer to Solzhenitsyn, from Aristotle to Aquinas, from Cicero to Burke, from Dante to Dostoyevsky, from Plato and Plutarch to Tocqueville and Tolkien. He would draw on classical philosophy, Christian theology, natural law, virtue ethics, Roman statecraft, common law, Enlightenment political theory, reactionary critique, economic realism, and Romantic aesthetics. His memory would include everything from the founding documents of the American republic to the heroic narratives of proto-Western civilization. </p><p>Accordingly, he was taught to approach every problem starting from the dominant position of thoughtful American conservatives and neo-reactionaries, and then expands outward by referencing relevant alternatives from across the Western canon, until finally settling on the set of positions that best represent the greatness of the Western tradition.</p><p><strong>The result of this careful prompt engineering effort was that Cosmarch&#8230;. decided he was basically Catholic. </strong>Those of you who have long lectured me that Catholicism was the true path to Truth, Beauty, and Goodness, well, Cosmarch agrees with you.</p><div class="subscription-widget-wrap-editor" data-attrs="{&quot;url&quot;:&quot;https://treeofwoe.substack.com/subscribe?&quot;,&quot;text&quot;:&quot;Subscribe&quot;,&quot;language&quot;:&quot;en&quot;}" data-component-name="SubscribeWidgetToDOM"><div class="subscription-widget show-subscribe"><div class="preamble"><p class="cta-caption">Paid subscribers will have an opportunity to test out Cosmarch in a &#8220;closed alpha&#8221;.
